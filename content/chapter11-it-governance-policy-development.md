## IT Policies

### Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

#### Purpose
- **Objective**: Define appropriate use of IT resources
- **Scope**: All users of organizational IT resources
- **Goal**: Protect organizational assets and ensure appropriate use
- **Benefit**: Clear guidelines for IT resource usage

#### Key Elements
- **Authorized use**: What constitutes appropriate use
- **Prohibited activities**: What activities are forbidden
- **Personal use**: Guidelines for personal use of IT resources
- **Consequences**: Penalties for policy violations
- **Monitoring**: Rights to monitor IT resource usage

#### Coverage Areas
- **Internet usage**: Guidelines for web browsing and downloads
- **Email usage**: Professional email communication standards
- **Social media**: Guidelines for social media use
- **File sharing**: Restrictions on file sharing
- **Security**: Security requirements and practices

### Information Security Policy

#### Overview
- **Purpose**: Establish framework for information security
- **Scope**: All information assets and processing
- **Objectives**: Confidentiality, integrity, availability
- **Compliance**: Regulatory and legal requirements

#### Policy Components
- **Asset classification**: Categorization of information assets
- **Access control**: Principles for access management
- **Incident response**: Procedures for security incidents
- **Business continuity**: Plans for service continuity
- **Compliance**: Adherence to standards and regulations

#### Implementation
- **Roles and responsibilities**: Clear assignment of security responsibilities
- **Training and awareness**: Education programs for staff
- **Monitoring and review**: Regular assessment of policy effectiveness
- **Updates**: Periodic review and updates to policy

### Data Classification Policy

#### Purpose
- **Objective**: Categorize data based on sensitivity and criticality
- **Scope**: All organizational data assets
- **Goal**: Apply appropriate protection based on data classification
- **Benefit**: Risk-based data protection approach

#### Classification Levels
- **Public**: Information that can be freely shared
- **Internal**: Information for internal use only
- **Confidential**: Sensitive information requiring protection
- **Restricted**: Highly sensitive information with strict controls

#### Handling Requirements
- **Labeling**: Mark data with appropriate classification
- **Access controls**: Apply access based on classification
- **Storage**: Secure storage requirements by classification
- **Transmission**: Secure transmission requirements
- **Disposal**: Secure disposal procedures by classification

### Password Policy

#### Purpose
- **Objective**: Ensure strong password security practices
- **Scope**: Password creation and management requirements
- **Goal**: Protect accounts from unauthorized access
- **Benefit**: Enhanced security through strong passwords

#### Key Requirements
- **Complexity**: Minimum length, character types
- **Expiration**: Password change intervals
- **History**: Prevention of password reuse
- **Storage**: Secure password storage requirements
- **Recovery**: Password reset procedures

#### Best Practices
- **Length**: Minimum 12 characters
- **Complexity**: Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols
- **Uniqueness**: Different passwords for different accounts
- **MFA**: Multi-factor authentication where possible

#### Security Requirements

- **Network security**: Secure network connections

## Governance Frameworks

### COBIT 2019: Framework Overview

#### Purpose
- **Objective**: Framework for IT governance and management
- **Focus**: Aligning IT with business goals
- **Development**: ISACA (Information Systems Audit and Control Association)
- **Approach**: Process-based framework with enablers

#### COBIT 2019 Components
- **Governance System**: Framework for governance and management
- **Design Factors**: Contextual factors affecting governance
- **Goals Cascade**: Linking stakeholder needs to IT goals
- **Core Model**: 40 management objectives organized in 5 domains
- **Performance Management**: Measuring and monitoring performance

#### Five Domains
- **Evaluate, Direct, and Monitor (EDM)**: Governance domain
- **Align, Plan, and Organize (APO)**: Strategic domain
- **Build, Acquire, and Implement (BAI)**: Solution domain
- **Deliver, Service, and Support (DSS)**: Service domain
- **Monitor, Evaluate, and Assess (MEA)**: Assurance domain

#### Governance vs Management
- **Governance (EDM)**: Directing and controlling the enterprise
- **Management (APO, BAI, DSS, MEA)**: Planning, building, running, and monitoring

#### Enablers
- **Processes**: How work gets done
- **Organizational structures**: Roles and responsibilities
- **Culture, ethics, and behavior**: Organizational culture
- **Principles, policies, and frameworks**: Guidance and direction
- **Information**: Data and information assets
- **Services, infrastructure, and applications**: Technology components
- **People, skills, and competencies**: Human resources

### ISO 38500: IT Governance Standard

#### Overview
- **Purpose**: International standard for IT governance
- **Focus**: Leadership and direction for IT use
- **Scope**: All types of organizations using IT
- **Approach**: Principles-based guidance

#### Six Principles
- **Responsibility**: Governing body is accountable for IT use
- **Strategy**: IT contributes to organizational strategy
- **Acquisition**: IT delivers required capabilities
- **Performance**: IT delivers benefits, manages risks, optimizes resource use
- **Conformance**: IT complies with requirements
- **Human behavior**: IT considers human behavior and ethics

#### Governance Body Responsibilities
- **Leadership**: Provide leadership for IT governance
- **Direction**: Set direction for IT use
- **Monitoring**: Monitor IT performance and conformance
- **Engagement**: Engage with stakeholders
- **Review**: Regularly review IT governance

### ITIL 4: Service Value System

#### Service Value Chain
- **Plan**: Governance and strategy alignment
- **Improve**: Continual improvement activities
- **Engage**: Interactions with stakeholders
- **Design and Transition**: Creating and implementing services
- **Obtain/Build**: Acquiring and building services
- **Deliver and Support**: Operational activities

#### Guiding Principles
- **Focus on value**: Everything should contribute to value creation
- **Start where you are**: Use existing resources and processes
- **Progress iteratively**: Small, manageable steps
- **Collaborate**: Work together and share information
- **Think holistically**: Consider the entire system
- **Keep it simple**: Minimize complexity
- **Optimize and automate**: Use technology to improve efficiency

## Service Management

### Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

#### Definition
- **Purpose**: Contractual agreements defining service expectations
- **Scope**: Service quality, availability, responsibilities
- **Goal**: Clear expectations between service provider and customer
- **Benefit**: Accountability and performance measurement

#### SLA Components
- **Service scope**: What services are covered
- **Performance metrics**: How service quality is measured
- **Responsibilities**: Who is responsible for what
- **Remedies**: What happens if SLAs are not met
- **Review process**: How SLAs are reviewed and updated

#### Common SLA Metrics
- **Availability**: Uptime percentage (e.g., 99.9%)
- **Response time**: Time to acknowledge requests
- **Resolution time**: Time to resolve issues
- **Performance**: System response times
- **Quality**: Accuracy, completeness of service

### Operational Level Agreements (OLAs)

#### Definition
- **Purpose**: Internal agreements between different IT departments
- **Scope**: Internal service delivery commitments
- **Goal**: Support achievement of SLAs
- **Benefit**: Internal accountability and coordination

#### Relationship to SLAs
- **Support**: OLAs support SLA achievement
- **Internal**: Internal to IT organization
- **Dependencies**: Identify internal service dependencies
- **Coordination**: Coordinate internal service delivery

#### Key Elements
- **Service commitments**: Internal service delivery commitments
- **Performance standards**: Internal performance expectations
- **Responsibilities**: Internal role and responsibility definitions
- **Monitoring**: Internal performance monitoring and reporting

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

#### Definition
- **Purpose**: Metrics to measure performance against objectives
- **Scope**: Quantifiable measurements of performance
- **Goal**: Track progress toward goals
- **Benefit**: Performance monitoring and improvement

#### KPI Characteristics
- **Specific**: Clearly defined and understood
- **Measurable**: Quantifiable and trackable
- **Achievable**: Realistic and attainable
- **Relevant**: Aligned with objectives
- **Time-bound**: Measured over specific time periods

#### IT Service KPIs
- **Incident resolution time**: Average time to resolve incidents
- **Service availability**: Percentage of time services are available
- **Customer satisfaction**: User satisfaction with services
- **Cost per transaction**: Cost of delivering services
- **First call resolution**: Percentage of issues resolved on first contact

### Service Catalog Management

#### Purpose
- **Objective**: Ensure accurate and consistent information about services
- **Scope**: Service catalog maintenance and publication
- **Goal**: Provide clear information about available services
- **Benefit**: Improved service visibility and understanding

#### Key Activities
- **Catalog maintenance**: Keep service information current
- **Information accuracy**: Ensure information is correct
- **Publication**: Make catalog available to users
- **Updates**: Manage changes to service information

## Change Management

### Change Advisory Board (CAB)

#### Purpose
- **Objective**: Review and approve changes to IT infrastructure
- **Scope**: Change evaluation and authorization
- **Goal**: Ensure changes are properly evaluated
- **Benefit**: Controlled change implementation

#### CAB Composition
- **Change manager**: Facilitates CAB meetings
- **Technical representatives**: Experts in affected areas
- **Business representatives**: Representatives of affected business areas
- **Security representative**: Security expertise
- **Operations representative**: Operations expertise

#### Meeting Process
- **Agenda**: Pre-planned meeting with change agenda
- **Evaluation**: Review change proposals and impacts
- **Decision**: Approve, reject, or defer changes
- **Documentation**: Record decisions and rationale

### Request for Change (RFC)

#### Purpose
- **Objective**: Formal request for changes to IT infrastructure
- **Scope**: All changes to configuration items
- **Goal**: Document and evaluate change requests
- **Benefit**: Controlled change process

#### RFC Components
- **Change description**: Detailed description of proposed change
- **Justification**: Business reason for the change
- **Impact assessment**: Effects on services and systems
- **Risk assessment**: Potential risks and mitigation
- **Implementation plan**: How change will be implemented
- **Backout plan**: How to reverse change if needed

### Change Types: Standard, Normal, Emergency

#### Standard Changes
- **Characteristics**: Pre-authorized, low-risk, frequently occurring
- **Process**: Expedited process with minimal evaluation
- **Examples**: Software patches, routine maintenance
- **Authorization**: Pre-approved by change authority

#### Normal Changes
- **Characteristics**: Standard evaluation and approval process
- **Process**: Full evaluation by CAB or designated authority
- **Examples**: System upgrades, new service implementations
- **Timeline**: Normal approval timeline

#### Emergency Changes
- **Characteristics**: Urgent, critical to business operations
- **Process**: Expedited approval process
- **Examples**: Critical security patches, system fixes
- **Follow-up**: Post-implementation review required

### Change Management Process

#### Pre-approval Activities
- **Initial assessment**: Preliminary change evaluation
- **Impact analysis**: Detailed impact assessment
- **Risk evaluation**: Risk identification and mitigation
- **Resource planning**: Identify required resources

#### Approval Activities
- **CAB review**: Change evaluation by advisory board
- **Authorization**: Formal approval decision
- **Scheduling**: Plan change implementation timing
- **Communication**: Notify stakeholders of approved changes

#### Post-approval Activities
- **Implementation**: Execute change according to plan
- **Verification**: Confirm change was implemented correctly
- **Documentation**: Update configuration management records
- **Review**: Evaluate change success and lessons learned

## Risk Management

### Risk Identification and Assessment

#### Risk Identification
- **Purpose**: Systematically identify potential risks
- **Methods**: Brainstorming, checklists, expert judgment
- **Scope**: All potential risks to objectives
- **Participants**: Stakeholders with relevant knowledge

#### Risk Assessment Process
- **Likelihood**: Probability of risk occurrence
- **Impact**: Consequences if risk occurs
- **Risk rating**: Combination of likelihood and impact
- **Prioritization**: Rank risks by significance

#### Risk Categories
- **Strategic**: Risks affecting strategic objectives
- **Operational**: Risks affecting day-to-day operations
- **Financial**: Risks affecting financial performance
- **Compliance**: Risks affecting regulatory compliance
- **Reputational**: Risks affecting organizational reputation

### Risk Analysis: Qualitative and Quantitative

#### Qualitative Risk Analysis
- **Purpose**: Prioritize risks based on probability and impact
- **Method**: Rating scales for probability and impact
- **Output**: Risk register with priority rankings
- **Benefits**: Quick risk prioritization

#### Quantitative Risk Analysis
- **Purpose**: Numerically analyze effect of risks
- **Method**: Statistical analysis and modeling
- **Output**: Probabilistic estimates of impact
- **Benefits**: Precise risk quantification

#### Risk Analysis Tools
- **Probability/impact matrix**: Grid for risk ranking
- **Monte Carlo simulation**: Statistical modeling technique
- **Decision trees**: Decision analysis tool
- **Sensitivity analysis**: Impact of variable changes

### Risk Mitigation Strategies

#### Risk Treatment Options
- **Avoid**: Eliminate risk by changing approach
- **Transfer**: Shift risk to another party
- **Mitigate**: Reduce probability or impact
- **Accept**: Acknowledge risk without action

#### Mitigation Techniques
- **Controls**: Implement preventive or detective controls
- **Contingency planning**: Prepare response plans
- **Diversification**: Spread risk across multiple areas
- **Insurance**: Transfer risk financially

#### Mitigation Planning
- **Responsibility**: Assign risk owners
- **Resources**: Allocate resources for mitigation
- **Timeline**: Establish implementation schedule
- **Monitoring**: Plan for ongoing monitoring

### Risk Monitoring and Review

#### Ongoing Monitoring
- **Key indicators**: Track risk indicators
- **Reporting**: Regular risk reporting
- **Review**: Periodic risk assessment updates
- **Adjustment**: Modify treatment plans as needed

#### Risk Register Maintenance
- **Updates**: Keep risk register current
- **New risks**: Identify emerging risks
- **Status tracking**: Monitor risk treatment progress
- **Lessons learned**: Incorporate lessons learned

### Practice Questions

1. What are the five domains of COBIT 2019?
2. Explain the difference between SLAs and OLAs.
3. What is the purpose of a CMDB?
4. Name three risk treatment options.
5. What does the acronym TCO stand for and what does it include?

### Answers

1. The five domains of COBIT 2019 are: Evaluate, Direct and Monitor (EDM); Align, Plan and Organize (APO); Build, Acquire and Implement (BAI); Deliver, Service and Support (DSS); Monitor, Evaluate and Assess (MEA).
2. SLAs (Service Level Agreements) are contracts between service provider and customer, while OLAs (Operational Level Agreements) are internal agreements between different IT departments to support SLA achievement.
3. A CMDB (Configuration Management Database) is a central repository that maintains information about configuration items and their relationships, supporting change and incident management.
4. The three main risk treatment options are: Avoid (eliminate risk), Transfer (shift risk to another party), Mitigate (reduce probability or impact).
5. TCO stands for Total Cost of Ownership, which includes all costs associated with acquiring, operating, maintaining, and disposing of IT assets over their lifecycle.
